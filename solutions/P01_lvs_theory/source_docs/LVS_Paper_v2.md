## Abstract

We propose the **Latent Vacuum Stationarity (LVS)** hypothesis: a conceptual unification framework suggesting that physical reality as observed may correspond to the set of stationary configurations—fixed points—within the full potential landscape of the quantum vacuum, and that time is not a fundamental dimension but an emergent artefact of local observation within an intrinsically atemporal structure.

The argument proceeds from a chain of established physical results: (i) the spacetime interval along null geodesics—the worldlines of photons mediating electromagnetic interaction—vanishes identically; (ii) macroscopic structure and observational access are overwhelmingly organized by electromagnetic interactions; (iii) the quantum vacuum is a superposition of all possible field configurations; (iv) the Wheeler–DeWitt equation describes a timeless universal state.

We synthesize these into a unified interpretive framework in which the apparent fine-tuning of physical constants, the measurement problem, and the emergence of classical reality are reinterpreted as consequences of fixed-point selection in a vacuum configuration space. The framework reinterprets mass as confined interaction energy defining the depth of a fixed point — illustrated by the March 2026 discovery of the Ξcc⁺ baryon at CERN, whose emergent mass exemplifies the fixed-point mechanism — gravitational time dilation as proximity to the atemporal substrate, and cosmological expansion as progressive actualization of the vacuum potential landscape.

We do not derive a new dynamical theory or propose new equations of motion. The present contribution is a **meta-interpretive synthesis**—a unifying conceptual lens through which several otherwise disconnected foundational problems may be read as aspects of a single underlying structure. We identify connections to the renormalization group fixed-point formalism, Wheeler's participatory universe, relational quantum mechanics, and PDE-based emergent dynamics, and outline experimental signatures that could, upon formalization, distinguish this framework from conventional interpretations.

**Keywords:** quantum vacuum, fixed-point structure, Wheeler–DeWitt equation, emergence of time, fine-tuning problem, measurement problem, latent space, stationarity, null geodesic, renormalization group, observation, mass-energy equivalence, cosmological expansion

## Scope and Status

The Latent Vacuum Stationarity (LVS) hypothesis, as presented here, is an **interpretive framework**, not a complete dynamical theory. We make this explicit at the outset:

- LVS does not derive the Standard Model or general relativity from first principles.
- LVS does not propose new particles, new forces, or modified equations of motion.
- LVS **does** propose a unifying interpretive principle—**stationarity in the vacuum configuration space**—that may connect several foundational tensions in modern physics within a single conceptual structure.

Throughout this paper, we carefully distinguish three levels of assertion:

1. **Established result** — experimentally verified or mathematically proven within accepted frameworks.
2. **Interpretive step** — a reading of established results that is defensible but not uniquely determined by the data.
3. **LVS extrapolation** — a consequence drawn within the LVS framework that goes beyond established interpretation and constitutes the novel contribution of this work.

These levels are marked explicitly in each section to allow the reader to evaluate the argumentative chain with full transparency.

The **problem of time**—the disappearance of the time variable in canonical quantum gravity, most starkly expressed in the Wheeler–DeWitt equation—challenges our deepest intuitions about the nature of change and causality [5, 6].

Time, spatial extent, and the apparent fine-tuning of constants emerge as artefacts of local observation within a fundamentally static structure.

## 2. The Null-Interval Mediator

### 2.1 The vanishing spacetime interval

**Established result.** In special relativity, the invariant spacetime interval along the worldline of a massive particle is:

> ds² = c²dt² − dx² = c²dτ²

where dτ is the proper time. For any entity travelling at v = c, the interval ds² = 0 identically. The worldline of a photon is a null geodesic—a path of zero invariant spacetime interval [7].

It is important to note, as a matter of mathematical rigour, that no valid inertial reference frame exists for an entity travelling at c. The Lorentz transformations are singular at v = c. Consequently, the common locution "from the photon's point of view" is a pedagogical shorthand, not a rigorous physical statement. What *is* rigorous is that the invariant interval along any null geodesic vanishes: emission and absorption are separated by zero spacetime interval as measured by the invariant metric.

**Interpretive step.** This means that the mediator of electromagnetic interaction is not naturally described by the same temporal parametrization as timelike observers. The photon does not experience a "journey" in any physically meaningful sense—its worldline connects two events with zero invariant separation.

**LVS extrapolation.** LVS interprets this as indicating that the fundamental carrier of electromagnetic interaction operates outside the spatiotemporal framework that massive observers take as fundamental. The atemporal character of null geodesics is not an anomaly but a glimpse of the deeper substrate.

### 2.2 The photon as dominant mediator of observation

**Established result.** In quantum electrodynamics (QED), the photon is the gauge boson of the U(1) electromagnetic symmetry. The Coulomb force holding electrons in atomic orbitals is, in the quantum field theory picture, mediated by virtual photon exchange [8]. The structural integrity of molecular bonds, the transmission of nerve impulses, the chemistry of metabolism—all are electromagnetic and photon-mediated.

The macroscopic world through which observers access and interact with reality is overwhelmingly organized by electromagnetic interactions, even though the full constitution of matter also depends on quantum chromodynamics (QCD, mediated by gluons), the weak interaction (mediated by W and Z bosons), and the Higgs mechanism [9]. The strong force confines quarks and generates ~98% of hadronic mass; the weak force governs radioactive decay; the Higgs field gives elementary fermions their bare mass. All four interactions, with their respective coupling constants, are constitutive of physical reality.

**Interpretive step.** The key observation for LVS is not that electromagnetism is the *only* relevant force, but that it dominates *observational access*. The vast majority of physical measurements—from human vision to particle detectors at CERN—rely on electromagnetic interaction. Even sensory modalities that seem non-optical (touch, hearing) ultimately transduce signals via electromagnetic forces at the molecular level. A blind person's tactile experience of reality is no less electromagnetic than a sighted person's visual experience—the interaction channel differs, but the underlying force is identical [10].

Photosensitivity is quasi-universal in the living world: bacteria possess photoreceptors, plants have phytochromes, and even abyssal organisms detect light. The observation channel varies; the underlying force does not.

**LVS extrapolation.** Our entire empirical access to the universe passes predominantly through a channel whose fundamental carrier has zero invariant spacetime interval. This creates a profound circularity: we observe reality through a mediator for which the spatiotemporal structure of that reality does not apply in the invariant sense.

### 2.3 Non-locality reinterpreted

**Established result.** The Bell inequality violations experimentally confirmed by Aspect, Zeilinger, and Clauser (Nobel Prize 2022) [11] demonstrate that entangled particles exhibit correlations that cannot be explained by local hidden variables. Quantum mechanics violates Bell-type inequalities, ruling out local realist interpretations.

**Interpretive step.** The "non-locality" of entanglement appears mysterious within a framework where spatial separation is fundamental. However, if the interactions constituting the entangled state are mediated along null geodesics with zero invariant interval, the apparent non-locality may reflect the null-interval character of the mediating substrate rather than a violation of any principle.

**LVS extrapolation.** Within LVS, non-locality is reinterpreted not as a violation of locality but as a manifestation of the atemporal, aspatial character of the underlying vacuum configuration space. The fixed-point structure does not respect the spatiotemporal separations that massive observers experience as fundamental.

## 3. The Quantum Vacuum as Configuration Space

### 3.1 The vacuum is not empty

**Established result.** In quantum field theory, the vacuum state |0⟩ is defined as the lowest-energy state of all quantum fields. It is not an absence of content but a superposition of all possible field configurations weighted by their action. Vacuum fluctuations—the continuous creation and annihilation of virtual particle-antiparticle pairs—are experimentally verified through the Casimir effect [12], the Lamb shift [13], and the anomalous magnetic moment of the electron [14].

**Interpretive step.** We propose to interpret the quantum vacuum as a **configuration space**—the space of all possible field configurations—which we refer to, by analogy with the terminology of dynamical systems and generative modelling, as a *latent space*. The analogy is functional, not formal: just as a latent space in a generative model contains all possible outputs as points in a continuous manifold, the quantum vacuum contains all possible field configurations as superposed potentialities.

To be precise about the mathematical candidates for this configuration space: depending on the formalization chosen, it may correspond to (a) the Hilbert space of the full quantum field theory, (b) the superspace of 3-geometries in canonical quantum gravity, (c) the solution space of the Wheeler–DeWitt constraint equation, or (d) the effective potential landscape of the vacuum sector. The present paper does not commit to a specific choice but argues that the *fixed-point structure* of this space—however formalized—is the physically relevant feature.

**LVS extrapolation.** The key insight is that this configuration space is **atemporal**: all configurations coexist as mathematical possibilities within a static structure. Time does not flow through this space; it emerges from within it.

### 3.2 Exhaustive coexistence without sequence

**Established result.** Quantum superposition allows all configurations to be co-present in the state vector simultaneously, not sequentially [15].

**LVS extrapolation.** The metaphor of a "quantum brute force" captures the intuition: an exhaustive exploration of the configuration space that does not unfold in time but simply *is*. The "winning" configurations—those that persist as observable reality—are not those that survived a temporal selection process, but those that constitute stationary points of the full potential landscape. This is natural selection without time: not survival of the fittest through competition, but existence of the stable through mathematical necessity.

## 4. Stationarity as the Criterion of Reality

### 4.1 Stationary states in quantum mechanics

**Established result.** In standard quantum mechanics, a stationary state is an eigenstate of the Hamiltonian operator:

> Ĥ |ψ⟩ = E |ψ⟩

The defining property of such states is **time-independence**: the probability distribution |ψ(**x**)|² does not change. The hydrogen atom in its ground state is not an electron "orbiting" a proton—it is a static probability distribution [16].

### 4.2 Fixed points of the renormalization group

**Established result.** In quantum field theory, the coupling constants of fundamental interactions are not truly constant—they "run" with energy scale, as described by the renormalization group (RG) equations. The RG flow is governed by the beta functions β(g), and the **fixed points** of this flow—defined by the condition β(g) = 0—correspond to scale-invariant theories with special physical significance [17].

The three gauge coupling constants of the Standard Model (electromagnetic, weak, and strong) run with energy, and near the scale of ~10¹⁶ GeV, they approximately converge—a near-unification that has motivated the Grand Unified Theory (GUT) research program for decades [18].

**Interpretive step.** The renormalization group is, mathematically, a theory of fixed points in the space of physical theories. The fact that physics already possesses a well-developed formalism for identifying stable configurations in coupling-constant space is significant for LVS.

**LVS extrapolation.** We propose that what the RG describes locally—the flow of couplings toward fixed points at specific energy scales—LVS elevates to a global ontological principle: observable reality corresponds to the fixed point of the full vacuum configuration space. The specific values of all coupling constants, particle masses, and symmetry structures of our universe are not free parameters requiring external explanation; they are the **coordinates of the fixed point** we inhabit.

This reframes the objection that "LVS neglects non-electromagnetic forces." On the contrary: the strong coupling constant αs, the Fermi constant GF, the Higgs coupling λ, and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant α are all properties of the same fixed point. They are what they are because this specific configuration is stationary. The fine-tuning problem becomes: why does a fixed point with these properties exist? And the answer, within LVS, is tautological—a fixed point exists because it satisfies its own stationarity conditions.

### 4.3 Case study: the neutrino mass as a near-zero fixed-point coordinate

**Established result.** The KATRIN experiment has established an upper bound on the neutrino mass of 0.8 eV/c² — billions of times less than the electron mass (0.511 MeV/c²) [18b]. Neutrino oscillation experiments (Nobel Prize 2015) proved that neutrinos have non-zero mass, but in the original Standard Model, neutrinos are strictly massless. The neutrino mass is therefore already a signal of physics beyond the Standard Model.

**Interpretive step.** The extreme smallness of the neutrino mass — non-zero but fantastically close to zero — is one of the most puzzling hierarchies in particle physics. The leading theoretical explanation is the **seesaw mechanism**: the observed neutrino mass m_ν is inversely proportional to a very large mass scale M (possibly near the GUT scale ~10¹⁵ GeV), via the relation m_ν ~ v²/M, where v is the Higgs vacuum expectation value. The lighter the neutrino, the heavier the unseen partner.

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, the neutrino mass is a coordinate of the vacuum fixed point — but one that sits very close to zero in the coupling-constant space. This is not accidental: the seesaw relation m_ν ~ v²/M is a **constraint between fixed-point coordinates**. The smallness of one coordinate (m_ν) is structurally linked to the largeness of another (M) through the stationarity conditions. The neutrino mass is not "fine-tuned to be small" — it is small because the fixed-point geometry relates it inversely to a large-scale parameter.

This illustrates a key feature of the LVS framework: the physical constants are not independent free parameters. They are coordinates in a high-dimensional fixed-point manifold, constrained by mutual relationships that arise from the stationarity conditions. The hierarchy between the neutrino mass and other fermion masses is not a mystery to be explained by external mechanisms — it is a geometric property of the fixed point. Furthermore, knowing the precise neutrino mass is vital for cosmology because it influences large-scale structure formation, the cosmic neutrino background, and the expansion rate — under LVS, this is natural: every fixed-point coordinate affects the global structure of the stationary configuration.

### 4.4 The LVS principle

We formalize:

> **Latent Vacuum Stationarity (LVS) Principle:** Observable physical reality corresponds to the set of stationary configurations—fixed points—of the quantum vacuum configuration space. A configuration is "real" if and only if it constitutes a time-independent, self-consistent solution of the full vacuum dynamics. Stability is not a property that some configurations happen to possess; it is the criterion of physical existence.

### 4.5 Fine-tuning dissolved

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, the fine-tuning problem dissolves. The physical constants are not free parameters that were "chosen" or "tuned." They are properties of a fixed point in the vacuum potential landscape. Asking why they have these values is equivalent to asking why a stable equilibrium is stable—it is a tautology, not a mystery [19].

This eliminates the need for both the designer hypothesis and the standard multiverse hypothesis. The configuration space contains all possible configurations, but only the fixed points manifest as coherent reality. Non-stationary configurations do not "exist elsewhere"—they simply never manifest.

### 4.6 Beyond the anthropic tautology

The conventional anthropic principle observes that we can only exist in a universe compatible with our existence—true but explanatorily vacuous. LVS goes further: the constants are not merely "compatible with" observers; they are *defined by* the fixed-point condition. An observer is a subsystem within a fixed point that is capable of registering other features of that fixed point. The relationship between constants and observers is not coincidental selection but structural co-definition.

## 5. The Emergence of Time

### 5.1 The Wheeler–DeWitt equation

**Established result.** The Wheeler–DeWitt equation, the central equation of canonical quantum gravity, takes the form:

> Ĥ |Ψ⟩ = 0

where |Ψ⟩ is the quantum state of the entire universe. This equation contains **no time derivative**. The state of the universe is static—it does not evolve. The "problem of time" in quantum gravity is precisely the challenge of recovering the manifest temporal character of our experience from a timeless fundamental equation [5, 6].

### 5.2 Time as relational ordering

**Established result.** The Page–Wootters mechanism [20] demonstrates that time can emerge as a relational parameter: when the universe is divided into a "clock" subsystem and a "rest" subsystem, the correlations between them can be parametrized by a monotonic variable that behaves as time for the subsystems, even though the global state is static. This mechanism has been experimentally demonstrated in optical systems [21].

**Interpretive step.** Time is not a fundamental dimension in which physics unfolds but a parameter describing correlations between subsystems within a globally static state.

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, this resolution is natural. The universal state is a fixed point in the vacuum configuration space, consistent with the Wheeler–DeWitt equation. Time emerges when a subsystem within this static structure correlates with another subsystem in a way that admits monotonic parametrization. This is analogous to a book: the text exists simultaneously on all pages, but a reader embedded within the book can only access it sequentially. The narrative does not "unfold"—the reading does.

The vanishing spacetime interval along null geodesics is then not an anomaly but a glimpse of the fundamental level—the regime where the relational parametrization we call "time" has not emerged. Massive observers, by contrast, are subsystems whose internal correlations generate the time parameter. **Time is the relational cost of being a local observer in an atemporal whole.**

### 5.3 Retrocausality reinterpreted

**Established result.** Wheeler's delayed-choice experiments [22] and their extensions [23] demonstrate that the choice of measurement made "now" can determine the behaviour of a photon in the "past."

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, retrocausality dissolves: there is no backward causation because there is no fundamental temporal ordering. The static structure has correlations between what observers at different "points" of their local time parameter would call past and future. Causality, like time, is emergent and local [24].

## 6. Mass as Confined Interaction Energy

### 6.1 The mass illusion

**Established result.** The proton has a rest mass of approximately 938 MeV/c². The three quarks composing it contribute only ~9 MeV/c² through the Higgs mechanism—less than 2% of the total. The remaining ~98% arises from the energy of gluon fields and quark kinetic energy confined within the proton by QCD [25]. A 70 kg human body contains roughly 1 kg of Higgs-derived "intrinsic" mass; the rest is interaction energy.

**Interpretive step.** Einstein's mass-energy equivalence, read right-to-left, says: mass is confined energy. The dominant form of this confined energy is interaction energy—gluons, virtual photons, and field energy trapped in stable configurations. Mass is not a substance; it is a *configuration*.

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, mass is the energy density of a fixed point. A massive object is a region of the vacuum configuration space where interaction energy is densely confined in a stationary configuration. The more confined the energy, the deeper the fixed point, the greater the mass. All four fundamental interactions—with their respective coupling constants—contribute to the fixed-point depth.

### 6.2 Case study: the Ξcc⁺ baryon as an alternative QCD fixed point

**Established result.** In March 2026, the LHCb collaboration at CERN announced the discovery of the Ξcc⁺ (Xi-cc-plus) baryon — a particle structurally similar to the proton but containing two charm quarks and one down quark instead of the proton's two up quarks and one down quark. Its measured mass is 3,619.97 ± 0.79 MeV/c², approximately four times the proton mass (938 MeV/c²). The discovery, made with a statistical significance of 7σ, was the first particle identification using the upgraded LHCb detector and only the second observation of a doubly charmed baryon [25b].

**Interpretive step.** A charm quark is approximately 500 times heavier than an up quark. Yet replacing the two up quarks in the proton with two charm quarks does not yield a particle 500 times heavier — it yields one only 4 times heavier. This dramatic nonlinearity demonstrates that the mass of a baryon is overwhelmingly determined by the QCD confinement dynamics (the gluon field energy), not by the intrinsic masses of the constituent quarks. The quarks set the boundary conditions; the strong-force dynamics determine the emergent mass.

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, the proton and the Ξcc⁺ are **two distinct fixed points of the same QCD potential landscape** — two different stationary solutions of the same field equations, with different boundary conditions (quark flavour content) but the same underlying dynamics. The proton is a shallow fixed point (938 MeV/c²); the Ξcc⁺ is a deeper one (3,620 MeV/c²). Both masses are emergent properties of their respective fixed-point configurations, not intrinsic properties of their parts.

This illuminates the broader LVS picture: the 80+ hadrons discovered at the LHC are not a chaotic zoo of unrelated particles. They are the **catalogue of fixed points** of QCD — the complete set of stationary configurations admitted by the strong-force potential landscape. Each hadron's mass, lifetime, and decay channels are determined by its fixed-point properties. The "particle zoo" is a fixed-point atlas.

This atlas extends beyond conventional baryons (3 quarks) and mesons (quark-antiquark). Since 2015, the LHCb experiment has discovered several **tetraquarks** (4-quark bound states) and **pentaquarks** (5-quark bound states) — exotic hadrons whose existence was theoretically permitted but long considered unlikely as stable configurations [25c]. The Tcc⁺ (a doubly charmed tetraquark observed in 2021), the Pc(4450)⁺ pentaquark states, and numerous Zc and Zb exotic resonances each represent a distinct stationary solution of the QCD field equations — a fixed point in the strong-force potential landscape with its own specific mass, quantum numbers, and decay properties.

Under LVS, the existence of these exotic states reveals the **topographic richness of the QCD fixed-point landscape**. The landscape contains not only the deep, stable valleys (proton: stable indefinitely; neutron: τ ≈ 15 minutes) but also shallow basins (exotic hadrons: τ ~ 10⁻²³ seconds) that are genuine fixed points but with minimal depth. The hierarchy of hadron lifetimes maps directly to the hierarchy of fixed-point depths:

| Particle | Type | Mass (MeV/c²) | Lifetime | Fixed-point depth |
|----------|------|---------------|----------|-------------------|
| Proton | Baryon (uud) | 938 | Stable (> 10³⁴ years) | Very deep |
| Neutron | Baryon (udd) | 940 | ~15 minutes | Deep |

*Table 1b. The QCD fixed-point hierarchy: from deep (stable) to shallow (ephemeral). All masses are emergent properties of their respective fixed-point configurations.*

The proton's extraordinary stability — it may never decay — reflects its position as the deepest baryon fixed point of QCD. The exotic hadrons' fleeting existence reflects their status as shallow fixed points: real solutions of the stationarity conditions, but minimally stable against perturbation. Under LVS, "existence" is not binary but graded: the depth of the fixed point determines the degree of manifestation.

Moreover, the fact that the Ξcc⁺ mass was predicted by lattice QCD calculations *before* its observation — and the measured value (3,620 MeV/c²) is consistent with these predictions — demonstrates that the stationarity conditions of the QCD field equations are sufficient to determine the emergent mass. The fixed-point conditions predict the constant. This is the LVS mechanism at work, verified experimentally in March 2026.

### 6.3 Gravitational time dilation as proximity to atemporality

**Established result.** General relativity predicts that clocks run slower near massive objects (gravitational time dilation), confirmed to extraordinary precision by GPS satellites, Pound-Rebka, and Gravity Probe A [26].

**Interpretive step.** The energy-momentum tensor curves spacetime geometry, and clocks in curved regions tick more slowly.

**LVS extrapolation.** A massive object is a particularly deep fixed point. Since time is an emergent parameter, and the fundamental substrate is atemporal, gravitational time dilation represents the approach toward the atemporal substrate. The closer to the core of a deep fixed point, the closer to the regime where time ceases to emerge.

### 6.4 Black holes as the limit case

**LVS extrapolation.** The black hole is a fixed point so deep that at the event horizon, time dilation becomes infinite. Under LVS, the interior of a black hole may represent a return to the unmanifested vacuum configuration space—a region where the conditions for the emergence of time and space are no longer satisfied.

The holographic principle—that the information content of a region is encoded on its boundary [27]—aligns with LVS: the horizon is the interface between manifested and unmanifested vacuum. Information is not "lost" inside the black hole; it returns to the configuration space from which it was never fundamentally separate.

We acknowledge that this reinterpretation, while conceptually suggestive, does not yet engage with the detailed structure of the information paradox (Page curve, scrambling time, firewall arguments) and requires formalization to generate distinguishable predictions.

## 7. Cosmological Expansion as Progressive Manifestation

### 7.1 The standard picture

**Established result.** The universe is expanding: the metric of spacetime stretches, carrying galaxies apart. The cosmic microwave background (CMB) represents photons emitted ~380,000 years after the Big Bang. Beyond the CMB lies the limit of our observable universe [28].

### 7.2 Expansion as actualization

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, space does not pre-exist and stretch—it is progressively actualized from the vacuum configuration space through interaction. Where no interaction has established a fixed-point structure, there is not empty space but unmanifested potential.

The analogy is closer to procedural generation than to fog of war: the terrain is not pre-existing and hidden, but generated as interaction extends the manifested network.

**A note on inflation.** In the standard model, the inflationary epoch involved metric expansion vastly exceeding the speed of light. This might appear to contradict a photon-mediated "front of manifestation." However, within LVS, the speed of light is not a constraint *on* actualization but a property *within* already-actualized spacetime. Inflation, under this reading, would represent a phase of rapid actualization of the vacuum configuration space—a cascade of fixed-point stabilization across a vast region of the potential landscape—prior to the establishment of the electromagnetic constraints that govern the subsequent epoch. The actualization front is not limited by c because c is a property of the manifested fixed point, not of the configuration space itself.

### 7.3 Dark energy as actualization tendency

**LVS extrapolation.** Dark energy, constituting ~68% of the universe's energy density [28], admits a reinterpretation as the intrinsic tendency of the vacuum configuration space to actualize—to extend the fixed-point structure wherever stationarity conditions can be satisfied at the boundary. The cosmological constant Λ would measure the actualization rate rather than a mysterious repulsive force.

We note that this reinterpretation is the most speculative element of the LVS framework and requires mathematical formalization to produce predictions distinguishable from standard ΛCDM cosmology.

### 7.4 The size question dissolved

Under LVS, "what is the size of the universe?" becomes a question about the extent of manifestation—how much of the vacuum configuration space has been actualized into fixed-point structure. The configuration space itself is not spatial; it has no size. Only the manifested subset has extent.

## 8. Observation and Fixed-Point Selection

### 8.1 Measurement as projection onto stationarity

**Established result.** In quantum mechanics, measurement projects the state onto an eigenstate of the measured observable [1].

**Interpretive step.** It is important to note that eigenstates of a measured observable are not necessarily stationary states (eigenstates of the Hamiltonian). Measuring position, for example, produces a localized state that immediately evolves. The relationship between measurement eigenstates and stationarity requires clarification.

**LVS extrapolation.** Under LVS, the measurement process involves two logically distinct steps: (1) projection onto an eigenstate of the observable (standard QM), and (2) the persistence of this outcome as a stable record in the macroscopic world, which *does* require stationarity of the recording apparatus and its environment. A measurement result is "real"—i.e., contributes to the classical world—only when it is registered in a stationary macroscopic configuration. The transient, non-stationary post-measurement quantum state is not itself the "reality" that LVS identifies with fixed points; it is the stable, decoherence-protected record that constitutes the fixed-point contribution [29].

### 8.2 The observer's role: constitutive, not causal

**LVS extrapolation.** The observer is not "creating" reality through a mental act. An observer is any subsystem capable of establishing stable internal correlations—any subsystem that is itself a fixed point capable of registering other fixed points. The observer is constitutive of reality in the sense that reality is a relational structure: fixed points defined with respect to interactions between subsystems [30, 31].

This aligns with Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics [30], where quantum states are always relative to an observer, and with QBism [31], where the quantum state encodes an agent's expectations. LVS offers a physical substrate for these interpretational frameworks: the relational structure of reality is the fixed-point structure of the vacuum.

### 8.3 The electromagnetic observation bias

**Interpretive step.** Our empirical access to reality is overwhelmingly electromagnetic. Dark matter—~27% of the universe's energy density—is invisible precisely because it does not interact electromagnetically [32].

**LVS extrapolation.** The "reality" we experience may not be the full fixed-point structure of the vacuum but only its electromagnetically accessible submanifold. Dark matter may represent fixed-point structures belonging to sectors of the configuration space that are orthogonal to our electromagnetic observation channel.

### 8.4 Wheeler's self-excited circuit

**LVS extrapolation.** Wheeler's "self-excited circuit" [33]—a universe that brings itself into existence through observation—finds a natural home in LVS. The fixed-point structure is self-referential: stationary configurations sustain subsystems (observers) whose interactions maintain the stationarity of the whole. The universe is a self-consistent fixed point—a structure that exists because it satisfies its own conditions of existence.

Several major experimental results from 2022–2026 bear directly on the LVS framework. We examine each through the lens of fixed-point stationarity, distinguishing established facts from LVS interpretation.

### 10.1 Antimatter falls like matter (CERN ALPHA, 2023)

**Established result.** The ALPHA experiment at CERN achieved the first direct observation that antihydrogen atoms fall downward under gravity, at a rate consistent with the gravitational acceleration of ordinary hydrogen. This definitively rules out the antigravity hypothesis for antimatter and confirms the equivalence principle of general relativity for antimatter [42].

**LVS interpretation.** Under LVS, gravitational behaviour is determined by the energy density of a fixed point — the depth of the stationary configuration in the vacuum landscape. An antiproton has the same mass (same confined energy) as a proton; it represents the same depth of fixed point, with opposite quantum numbers but identical energy content. LVS therefore predicts identical gravitational response for matter and antimatter, since the fixed-point depth — not the particle/antiparticle distinction — determines spacetime curvature. The ALPHA result is a necessary consequence of the LVS framework, not a surprise.

### 10.2 Stochastic gravitational wave background (NANOGrav, 2023)

**Established result.** The NANOGrav collaboration, using decades of pulsar timing data, detected a stochastic background of gravitational waves at nanohertz frequencies — a cosmic "hum" likely produced by the inspiral of supermassive black hole binaries across the universe's history [43].

**LVS interpretation.** Under LVS, the spacetime fixed point is not perfectly rigid. Like any physical fixed point, it admits low-energy perturbations around the stationary configuration — oscillations that, in the language of general relativity, are gravitational waves. The stochastic background detected by NANOGrav is the fluctuation spectrum of the cosmological fixed point itself. It is not random noise; it is a signature of the fixed-point topology. The frequency spectrum and amplitude of this background encode information about the structure of the vacuum landscape near our fixed point — specifically, about the deepest sub-fixed-points (supermassive black holes) and their mutual interactions within the global stationary configuration.

### 10.3 Muon anomalous magnetic moment (Fermilab g-2, 2021–2023)

**Established result.** The g-2 experiment at Fermilab measured the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon with unprecedented precision, finding a value that initially appeared to deviate from Standard Model predictions. The status of this anomaly is evolving: lattice QCD calculations have partially reduced the discrepancy, and the situation remains under active investigation [44].

**LVS interpretation.** If the anomaly is confirmed, it implies that the muon couples to degrees of freedom not contained in the Standard Model — indicating that our current description of the vacuum fixed point is incomplete. Under LVS, this would mean that the fixed-point structure has dimensions we have not yet mapped: additional stationary configurations or coupling relationships that modify the muon's interaction with the vacuum. The anomaly would be a signal of **uncharted fixed-point topology**, not random deviation.

If the anomaly is resolved by improved Standard Model calculations, this equally supports LVS: it confirms that the stationarity conditions encoded in the Standard Model are sufficient to determine the muon's properties to extraordinary precision — the fixed point predicts the constant correctly.

Both outcomes are compatible with LVS. What LVS does not accommodate is a genuinely random, unexplainable deviation — which no physical framework accommodates.

### 10.4 Rare Higgs decay H → Zγ (CERN ATLAS/CMS, 2023)

**Established result.** The ATLAS and CMS collaborations at CERN observed the rare decay of the Higgs boson into a Z boson and a photon, with a measured rate consistent with Standard Model predictions [45].

**LVS interpretation.** Every decay channel of the Higgs boson — with its specific branching ratio — is a property of the vacuum fixed point. The branching ratios are not free parameters; they are determined by the coupling constants (themselves fixed-point coordinates) through the stationarity conditions of the electroweak sector. The observation of the rare H → Zγ channel at the predicted rate confirms that the fixed-point conditions determine even the most delicate properties of the Higgs sector. Each new confirmed branching ratio is an additional consistency check on the fixed-point structure.

### 10.5 Early massive galaxies (JWST, 2022–2024)

**Established result.** The James Webb Space Telescope has discovered galaxies that are unexpectedly massive and structured at very early cosmic times — within a few hundred million years of the Big Bang. These observations challenge standard models of galaxy formation, which predict that such structures require significantly longer timescales to assemble through hierarchical accretion [46].

**LVS interpretation.** This may be the observational result most naturally accommodated by LVS. Under the standard cosmological model, structure formation is a slow temporal process: matter accretes gravitationally over billions of years, gradually building larger structures. The JWST observations suggest that this temporal narrative is inadequate.

Under LVS, structure formation is not primarily a temporal process. Galaxies, stars, and large-scale structures are **fixed points of the vacuum landscape** — stationary configurations of matter and energy that manifest wherever the stationarity conditions are locally satisfied. If the conditions for a massive galaxy fixed point are met early in the actualization history of the vacuum, the structure manifests directly — it does not need to be "built" through slow accretion. The early universe, under LVS, is not a period of slow construction but a phase of rapid **fixed-point crystallization**: the cascade of stabilization through the vacuum landscape produces complex structures as soon as the local conditions permit.

The JWST galaxies are not "too early" — the accretion timescale is simply not the relevant variable. The relevant variable is whether the stationarity conditions for a galaxy-scale fixed point are satisfied, and the JWST observations suggest they are satisfied much earlier than temporal models predict.

### 10.6 Bell inequality violations at scale (Nobel Prize 2022)

**Established result.** The 2022 Nobel Prize in Physics (Aspect, Clauser, Zeilinger) recognized decades of experiments definitively proving that quantum entanglement violates Bell inequalities — ruling out local hidden variable theories and confirming that spatially separated particles exhibit correlations with no local causal explanation [11].

**LVS interpretation.** This result is already integrated into the LVS framework (Section 2.4). Non-locality appears paradoxical only within a framework where spatial separation is fundamental. Under LVS, the fixed-point structure of the vacuum does not respect the local spatiotemporal separations that massive observers experience. Entangled particles are correlated because they are features of a single stationary configuration — they were never separated at the level of the vacuum fixed point. The Nobel 2022 experiments validate the foundational premise of LVS: the spatiotemporal framework is emergent, not fundamental.

## 11. Experimental Signatures and Predictions

For the LVS hypothesis to qualify as scientific rather than purely philosophical, it must, upon formalization, generate distinguishable predictions. We identify several directions where LVS-motivated research could yield testable signatures:

**11.1 Renormalization group fixed-point structure.** If the Standard Model coupling constants are coordinates of a vacuum fixed point, there should exist mathematical relationships between them derivable from stationarity conditions on the full vacuum potential landscape. The near-convergence of gauge couplings at the GUT scale [18] is suggestive; LVS predicts that exact convergence (or a precise pattern of near-convergence) is a fixed-point property, not a coincidence. Lattice QCD and precision electroweak measurements could constrain this.

**11.2 Decoherence signatures.** The transition from quantum superposition to classical definiteness should, under LVS, exhibit signatures consistent with projection onto a stationary submanifold, potentially distinguishable from the gradual loss of coherence predicted by standard decoherence theory.

**11.3 Cosmological fixed-point imprints.** The large-scale structure should bear imprints of fixed-point selection—possibly detectable as non-Gaussian features in the CMB or as specific patterns in running coupling constants converging toward a fixed point across energy scales.

**11.4 Temporal emergence thresholds.** If time is emergent, there may exist a scale—likely near the Planck scale—below which temporal parametrization breaks down, potentially observable through modified dispersion relations in high-energy astrophysical observations [37].

**11.5 Dark energy and actualization rate.** If expansion is progressive actualization, its acceleration should correlate with properties of the manifested fixed-point boundary—a prediction potentially distinguishable from a simple cosmological constant through precision expansion-history measurements.

**11.6 Black hole information signatures.** If black hole interiors represent unmanifested configuration space, Hawking radiation should encode fixed-point structure properties, potentially detectable in correlations between early and late radiation [38].

We acknowledge that these remain **directions for formalization** rather than quantitative predictions in their current form. The development of a mathematical model—most likely within the framework of background-independent quantum gravity—is required to produce specific numerical predictions.

## 12. Discussion

### 12.1 Relationship to existing frameworks

LVS is a **meta-interpretive framework**—a way of reading the existing formalism of quantum field theory and quantum gravity that unifies several otherwise disconnected observations. It shares elements with:

- The **Hartle–Hawking no-boundary proposal** [39]: the universe as a timeless mathematical object.
- **Barbour's timeless physics** [40]: configurations rather than histories as fundamental.
- **Tegmark's mathematical universe** [41]: mathematical structures as the substance of reality.
- **Rovelli's relational QM** [30]: properties as relational, not absolute.
- The **renormalization group** [17]: fixed points as organizing principles of physical theories.

The distinctive contribution of LVS is the identification of **stationarity**—the fixed-point condition—as the single criterion that selects the realized configuration from the configuration space of possibilities, and the synthesis of this principle across the measurement problem, fine-tuning, the problem of time, mass, gravity, and cosmology.

### 12.2 The consciousness question

LVS deliberately avoids making consciousness a foundational element. The framework requires observers only in the minimal sense of subsystems capable of forming stable correlations. However, LVS is compatible with stronger claims: if consciousness is itself a fixed-point phenomenon—a self-referential stable configuration of information processing—it would naturally arise where stationarity conditions support complex self-referential structures. Under LVS, "why is there consciousness?" transforms into "why are self-referential fixed points a feature of the vacuum landscape?"—a question amenable to mathematical investigation.

### 12.3 The designer question reframed

The fine-tuning argument for an intelligent designer is dissolved under LVS, but not dismissively. The constants are not accidental, nor chosen. They are *necessary*—properties of a self-consistent fixed point. The universe is its own explanation. The elegance attributed by theists to a designer and by atheists to chance is, under LVS, the mathematical property of a fixed point: self-consistency.

### 12.4 Limitations and open questions

The principal limitation of LVS is its **qualitative character**. The conceptual synthesis is compelling, but formalization within a specific mathematical framework is required to generate precise quantitative predictions. Key open questions include:

- **Which mathematical space?** The vacuum configuration space must be precisely defined (Hilbert space, superspace, effective potential landscape, or solution space of constraint equations).
- **Which fixed point?** LVS explains *why* constants are stable but does not yet predict *which* fixed point corresponds to our universe.
- **Inflation formalization.** The reinterpretation of inflation as rapid actualization requires a concrete mechanism for how actualization rate relates to known inflationary dynamics.
- **Black hole details.** The connection to the information paradox requires engagement with the Page curve, scrambling time, and entanglement entropy calculations.
- **Quantitative predictions.** The experimental signatures outlined in Section 10 must be converted to specific observables with calculable amplitudes.

The development of a **toy model**—for example, a PDE system in which stable fronts propagate through an initially undifferentiated field, with emergent relational time and fixed-point-determined "constants"—would represent a significant step toward formalization.

## 13. Conclusion

We have presented the Latent Vacuum Stationarity hypothesis: a conceptual unification suggesting that the measurement problem, the fine-tuning problem, the problem of time, the nature of mass, gravitational time dilation, and cosmological expansion may be aspects of a single underlying structure. The quantum vacuum is an atemporal configuration space; reality is the subset of stationary configurations—the fixed points—of this space; time is an emergent relational parameter within a fundamentally static whole; mass is confined interaction energy defining the depth of a fixed point; and cosmological expansion is the progressive actualization of the configuration space through interaction.

The chain of reasoning is built on established physical facts: the vanishing spacetime interval along null geodesics, the electromagnetic organization of macroscopic structure, the non-emptiness of the quantum vacuum, the timelessness of the Wheeler–DeWitt equation, the dominance of interaction energy over intrinsic mass, and the fixed-point structure of the renormalization group. What is novel is not any individual link but the chain itself, and the principle it suggests: that **stability and existence are the same thing**.

This framework is parsimonious—it requires no designer, no physically realized multiverse, and no consciousness as a causal primitive. It is consistent with established physics while offering a unified perspective on problems that have resisted solution in isolation.

We close with a reformulation of Wheeler's dictum. Where Wheeler proposed "it from bit"—information as the foundation of physical existence—LVS proposes **"it from fix": reality from fixed point**. The universe exists not because it was created, designed, or selected, but because it is the configuration that satisfies its own conditions of existence. It is the answer that is its own question.
